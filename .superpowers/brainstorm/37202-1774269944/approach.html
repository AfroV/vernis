connect.html — Design Approach

How should the page be structured and what's the visual tone?

A

Split Card Layout

Two large cards side by side: WiFi (left) and Bluetooth (right). Each card has its own icon, status indicator, instructions, and action button. The pairing PIN overlay appears as a centered modal on top.

Pros

  • Clean, balanced — both methods equally prominent
  • Works well on wide screens (HDMI TV) and narrow (phone)
  • Easy to extend with more methods later

Cons

  • Two big cards might feel sparse on a phone
B

Tabbed Panel

A single centered panel with two tabs at the top: WiFi | Bluetooth. Each tab reveals its connection flow. Active tab has an accent underline. PIN display replaces the BT tab content when pairing.

Pros

  • Compact — works great on 720x720 Pi screen
  • Familiar pattern (settings-style)
  • PIN display feels natural in the tab content area

Cons

  • Only shows one method at a time
C

Stacked Sections (Recommended)

Full-width page with Vernis logo at top, then a WiFi section (QR code + IP), then a Bluetooth section (device name + pair button + PIN area). Scrollable on small screens. Premium feel with glass cards and subtle animations.

Pros

  • Both methods visible at once without feeling cramped
  • Natural reading flow — WiFi first (primary), BT second (fallback)
  • PIN display integrates into the BT section directly
  • Logo at top reinforces brand — feels like a premium product page

Cons

  • Needs scroll on 720px screen (but minimal)
